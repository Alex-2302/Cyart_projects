# Network Scanning – Nmap

## Overview

This task involved performing network scanning on a target system (Metasploitable2) using Nmap to identify open ports, running services, and their versions. Service enumeration was also carried out to gather detailed information about exposed services.

---

## What was done

A scan was conducted to discover active ports and services on the target machine. The results helped identify multiple services such as FTP, SSH, HTTP, and SMB that were running and accessible.

---

## Why it was done

The purpose of this activity was to understand the attack surface of the target system. By identifying open ports and services, it becomes easier to determine possible entry points that could be exploited during a security assessment.

---

## Importance

Network scanning is a fundamental step in Red Teaming and penetration testing. It helps in detecting exposed and outdated services that may contain vulnerabilities. This information is crucial for both attackers and defenders, as it supports vulnerability assessment and system hardening.

---

## Scan Insight

Different scanning techniques provide different levels of detail. Stealth scanning is less detectable and faster, while aggressive scanning provides deeper insights such as operating system details and service configurations but is more likely to be detected.

---

## Conclusion

The scanning process successfully identified exposed services and potential risks in the system. This highlights the importance of regular network scanning to improve security and reduce vulnerabilities.
